The Science of Soulmates: Understanding Ashtakoota in Kundali Milan

Published: 1 March 2026 at 6:00 am | By Nikesh Maurya | 8 min

The Foundation of Vedic Match Making

In the Vedic tradition, marriage is viewed as more than just a social contract; it is the union of two karmic paths. Kundali Milan, the ancient practice of horoscope matching, serves as a spiritual and psychological diagnostic tool to determine if two individuals can sustain a harmonious life together. While modern dating focuses on shared interests and physical attraction, Vedic astrology looks deeper into the Gunas—the inherent qualities of the soul.

The primary goal of Kundali Milan is to ensure that the couple's energies are complementary. When the planetary vibrations of two people align, they support each other’s growth. When they clash, it can lead to constant friction, health issues, or financial instability. The most widely used system for this assessment is the Ashtakoota, or the eight-fold test.

Decoding the Eight Gunas (Ashtakoota)

The Ashtakoota system assigns points (Gunas) to eight different aspects of compatibility, totaling 36 points. A score above 18 is generally considered acceptable, while a score above 25 indicates an excellent match.

Varna, Vashya, and Tara

  • Varna (1 Point): This measures the ego compatibility and the spiritual development of the couple. It ensures that both partners have a similar outlook on their work and social responsibilities.
  • Vashya (2 Points): This looks at the power dynamic. It calculates who will be more dominant in the relationship and whether that dominance will be accepted gracefully by the other.
  • Tara (3 Points): This relates to the birth star (Nakshatra) compatibility and indicates the health and longevity of the partners.

Yoni, Maitri, and Gana

  • Yoni (4 Points): This measures physical and sexual compatibility. It is symbolized by different animals, and the test ensures that the biological 'natures' of the couple are not instinctively hostile to one another.
  • Maitri (5 Points): This is one of the most important factors, as it measures planetary friendship. It reflects the mental compatibility and the ability of the couple to communicate as friends.
  • Gana (6 Points): This determines the temperament. It categorizes individuals into Deva (Divine), Manushya (Human), or Rakshasa (Demonic) temperaments. Matching Ganas ensures that the couple's basic nature and behavior patterns are in sync.

Bhakoot and Nadi

  • Bhakoot (7 Points): This relates to the relative position of the Moon signs. It influences the emotional bond and the prosperity of the couple. A poor Bhakoot score is often associated with 'Bhakoot Dosha,' which can lead to emotional distance.
  • Nadi (8 Points): The most significant test, Nadi, deals with the genetic and physiological compatibility. It is vital for the health of the couple and the well-being of their future children. A 'Nadi Dosha' is often taken very seriously in Vedic astrology.

Why 18 Points Isn't Always Enough

While the 36-point system provides a quick snapshot, a truly expert astrologer knows that the score is not the final word. There are several factors that can override a low score or spoil a high one. For instance, Manglik Dosha (the placement of Mars) must be analyzed separately. If one partner is a Manglik and the other is not, it can create significant turbulence regardless of the Guna score.

Furthermore, the strength of the 7th house (the house of marriage) and the position of Venus (for men) and Jupiter (for women) must be evaluated. A high Ashtakoota score cannot save a marriage if the individual charts show a strong denial of marital happiness. Conversely, a couple with a score of 16 might lead a wonderful life if their 7th house lords are well-placed and they share a strong Atmakaraka (soul planet) connection.

The Role of Mars and Venus in Modern Love

In the contemporary world, where individual autonomy is highly valued, the role of Venus (desire and romance) and Mars (passion and drive) has become even more critical. While Ashtakoota focuses heavily on the Moon (emotions and subconscious), the physical and passionate side of a relationship is governed by these two planets. A balanced Kundali Milan today must look at how these planets interact to ensure that the 'spark' remains alive over decades of marriage.

We also look at the Navamsha Chart (D9), which is the fruit of the tree. While the main birth chart (D1) shows the promise of a relationship, the D9 chart reveals the actual reality of the marriage as it unfolds over time. If the D9 chart is strong, the couple will have the internal strength to overcome any external challenges.

Conclusion: A Roadmap, Not a Rulebook

Vedic compatibility is a profound science that offers a roadmap for a successful union. It helps couples understand where they will naturally flow together and where they might need to put in extra effort. It is not about finding a 'perfect' person, but about finding a person whose imperfections do not clash with your own. By understanding the Gunas and the planetary influences, couples can build a foundation of mutual respect and spiritual growth.
