Vedic Astrology Forecast: Planetary Positions and Transits for March 5, 2026

Published: 5 March 2026 at 6:00 am | By Nikesh Maurya | 7 min

The Celestial Landscape: Overview of March 5, 2026

As we navigate the cosmic currents of March 5, 2026, the heavens present a remarkably rare and potent configuration. In Vedic Astrology (Jyotish), the positioning of the Grahas (planets) provides a roadmap for the collective karma of the moment. Today is characterized by a unique 'Double Exaltation'—a period where both the Great Benefic, Jupiter, and the planet of love and luxury, Venus, reside in their highest states of dignity. This alignment creates a spiritual and material bridge, offering opportunities for profound growth, healing, and ethical advancement.

Major Planetary Placements and Their Significance

Sun in Aquarius: The Visionary Soul

Today, the Sun (Surya) continues its transit through Aquarius (Kumbha Rashi). In this Saturn-ruled sign, the Sun sheds its ego-centric nature and focuses on the collective good. This placement encourages us to look beyond personal gain and consider our role within the larger community. It is a time for innovation, social reform, and intellectual breakthroughs. However, because the Sun is in the sign of its enemy, Saturn, there may be a slight tension between individual authority and group dynamics.

Moon in Virgo: Practicality and Precision

The Moon (Chandra) resides in Virgo (Kanya Rashi) today, transiting through the Nakshatras of Uttara Phalguni or Hasta. This placement brings a grounded, analytical, and service-oriented energy to our emotional state. While the Sun in Aquarius dreams of the future, the Moon in Virgo provides the practical tools to build it. It is an excellent day for organizing, focusing on health and wellness, and attending to the finer details of any project.

The Power of Exalted Jupiter in Cancer

One of the most significant features of 2026 is Jupiter (Guru) transiting through Cancer (Karka Rashi), its sign of exaltation. Today, Jupiter’s influence is at its peak, radiating wisdom, expansion, and divine grace. This placement fosters a deep sense of compassion and protection. It is a 'Pushkara Navamsha' period for many, making it an ideal time for spiritual initiation, financial planning, and strengthening family bonds.

Significant Transits and Yogas

Venus in Pisces: The Height of Devotion

Adding to the day's auspiciousness, Venus (Shukra) is currently exalted in Pisces (Meena Rashi). When Venus is in Pisces, love transcends the physical and becomes a spiritual experience. This transit enhances creativity, empathy, and artistic expression. The combination of an exalted Jupiter and an exalted Venus creates a Saraswati Yoga-like influence, favoring those involved in the arts, music, and higher learning.

Saturn in Pisces: Spiritual Discipline

Saturn (Shani) is also transiting through Pisces, sharing the sign with Venus. While Venus seeks pleasure, Saturn seeks penance. This conjunction forces us to find a balance between our desires and our duties. It brings a sobering, disciplined approach to our spiritual practices, ensuring that our growth is rooted in reality rather than escapism.

Rahu and Ketu: The Karmic Axis

The nodes of the Moon, Rahu and Ketu, are positioned in Aquarius and Leo respectively. Rahu in Aquarius amplifies the desire for technological advancement and unconventional social structures, while Ketu in Leo asks us to detach from the need for personal recognition and applause. This axis highlights the struggle between the individual 'I' and the collective 'We'.

Impact on Collective Consciousness

The planetary positions of March 5, 2026, suggest a turning point for global ethics and humanitarian efforts. With Mars (Mangal) in Taurus (Vrishabha), there is a steady, determined energy applied to financial stability and resource management. The overall energy of the day is one of 'Practical Idealism.' We are encouraged to dream big (Sun in Aquarius, Venus in Pisces) but to execute those dreams with meticulous care (Moon in Virgo, Mars in Taurus).

This is a day to honor your teachers, engage in acts of charity, and refine your daily routines. The exalted benefics provide a safety net, making it a favorable time for starting new ventures that have a foundation of integrity and service.
